TUSCANY: Where art, architecture, and natural beauty converge

Why Tuscany?

Tuscany is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, with cities like Florence, Siena, and Pisa housing world-class art, architecture, and historical landmarks. Florence alone is home to masterpieces by Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Botticelli, among others.

Tuscany's countryside has picturesque landscapes of rolling hills and vineyards. It's the birthplace of Chianti, Brunello di Montalcino, and other renowned wines.

Attractions

Popular attractions in Tuscany

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo offers a panoramic view of Florence, Italy. Surrounded by lush gardens, the piazza is adorned with replicas of Michelangelo's masterpieces, such as the David statue. Situated atop a hill on the south bank of the Arno River, this enchanting square serves as a popular gathering spot for locals and tourists alike.

Leaning Tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is famous for its unintended tilt. Built over two centuries starting in 1173, its lean is due to unstable ground. Despite efforts to correct it, the tilt remains. This freestanding bell tower of the nearby cathedral complex attracts millions of visitors annually, not only for its structural anomaly but also for its stunning medieval architecture.

Address

Piazza del Duomo, 56126 Pisa PI, Italy

Learn More

Ponte Vecchio

The Ponte Vecchio, in Florence, Italy, is a historic bridge spanning the Arno River. Dating back to medieval times, it's renowned for its unique shops built along its length, originally occupied by butchers but now housing jewelers, art dealers, and souvenir sellers. The bridge's distinct architectural style, characterized by three segmental arches, attracts tourists and artists alike.

Address

Ponte Vecchio, 50125 Firenze FI, Italy
